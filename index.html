The Why

Why does the YCJA exist?

The YCJA knows teens are still growing and still learning. So it does not just copy the adult system. It tries to hold youth accountable in a way that is fair and actually helps.

Different from adults

The youth system is separate because young people have different maturity levels need more support and usually have a better chance to change.

Rehabilitation

The YCJA pushes schools families lawyers judges and programs to help youth learn from it fix harm and not do it again.

Reintegration

A big goal is getting youth back into normal life like school work counseling family support and better choices.

The How

What usually happens when a youth gets in trouble?

For less serious non violent offences the law often wants an outside court response first. That can be a warning a caution a referral or an extrajudicial sanction instead of sending a teen right into court.

1

Police contact

A youth who gets arrested or detained has to be told right away that they can talk to a lawyer.

2

Parent notice

If the youth is detained or has to go to court a parent needs to be told as soon as possible.

3

Extrajudicial option

For a first time non violent offence an outside court measure might be enough to hold the youth accountable and fix some harm.

4

Making things right

The youth might apologize return stolen stuff do community service go to counseling or take part in restorative justice.

Example of an Extrajudicial Measure

Imagine a 16 year old gets caught shoplifting. Instead of a full court process police or Crown might choose an outside court response if it is non violent and the youth takes responsibility.

  • Write an apology or join a restorative meeting.
  • Return or pay back what was taken.
  • Do community service hours.
  • Go to a program about choices theft or conflict.
Privacy and Records

What happens to a youth criminal record?

The YCJA gives youth more privacy than adults. A youth record is not the same as an adult record and only certain people can access it for certain amounts of time.

Identity protection

In most situations the public cannot publish a youth name or details that identify them. That helps protect rehabilitation and stops one mistake from following them forever.

Who can access records?

Access is limited. Police the court the youth the youth lawyer parents or guardians and some justice workers can access records in certain situations. It is not open to everyone forever.

Important reality

A youth record does not just vanish the second someone turns 18. It depends on the result of the case how serious it was and if they get in trouble again before the access period ends.

Bottom line

The YCJA still holds youth accountable but it tries not to let one mistake define their whole future. Privacy and limited records are part of helping a young person get back into life.

FAQ

Questions teens actually ask

These answers are in plain language so you can say them in class without sounding too formal.

Can I talk to a lawyer if I am arrested?

Yes. Under the YCJA a young person who is arrested or detained has to be told right away about their right to a lawyer and has to get a chance to contact one. In Ontario Legal Aid Ontario can help eligible youth.

Do my parents have to be told?

Usually yes. If a youth is arrested and detained or if they get a summons appearance notice or undertaking a parent has to be notified as soon as possible.

Will everyone know my name forever?

Usually not. The YCJA normally protects a youth identity from publication and youth records have limited access periods instead of staying open like adult records.
