The Why

Why does the YCJA exist?

The YCJA knows teens are still learning. It is different from the adult system and tries to be fair and helpful.

Different from adults

Young people need more support and have more time to change.

Rehabilitation

The YCJA wants youth to learn from what happened and fix harm.

Reintegration

A big goal is helping youth get back into normal life.

The How

What usually happens when a youth gets in trouble?

For less serious non violent offences the law often uses an outside court response first.

1

Police contact

A youth who gets arrested or detained has to be told right away they can talk to a lawyer.

2

Parent notice

If the youth is detained or has to go to court a parent needs to be told as soon as possible.

3

Extrajudicial option

For a first time non violent offence this might be enough to hold the youth accountable.

4

Making things right

The youth might apologize return stolen stuff do community service go to counseling or join restorative justice.

Example of an Extrajudicial Measure

Imagine a 16 year old gets caught shoplifting. Police or Crown might choose an outside court response if it is non violent and the youth takes responsibility.

  • Write an apology or join a restorative meeting.
  • Return or pay back what was taken.
  • Do community service hours.
  • Go to a program about choices or conflict.
Privacy and Records

What happens to a youth criminal record?

The YCJA gives youth more privacy than adults and only certain people can see a youth record.

Identity protection

In most situations the public cannot publish a youth name or details that identify them.

Who can access records?

Police the court the youth the youth lawyer parents or guardians and some justice workers can see records in some situations.

Important reality

A youth record does not just disappear at 18. It depends on the case and if they get in trouble again.

Bottom line

The YCJA still holds youth accountable but tries not to let one mistake define everything.

FAQ

Questions teens actually ask

These answers are simple and easy to say in class.

Can I talk to a lawyer if I am arrested?

Yes. Under the YCJA a young person who is arrested or detained has to be told right away about their right to a lawyer and gets a chance to contact one.

Do my parents have to be told?

Usually yes. If a youth is arrested and detained or if they get a summons appearance notice or undertaking a parent has to be told as soon as possible.

Will everyone know my name forever?

Usually not. The YCJA normally protects a youth identity from publication and youth records are more private than adult ones.
